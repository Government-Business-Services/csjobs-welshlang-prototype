      Crynodeb

      The People and Workplace directorate delivers a comprehensive HR service to the department. We offer a professional, expert and valued HR, workplace and security service to our colleagues through the Department and also work with the Arms Length Bodies DCMS sponsors.

      The directorate is split into 4 distinct teams that work together to ensure a smooth and efficient service is delivered.

      You will be based in the People Strategy and Development Team. We look after areas such as organisational development, diversity and inclusion, HR policy, pay and reward as well as people analytics.

      As a member of People and Workplace you can expect investment in your development, whether you are in the property, security, HR, analysis, comms or another civil service profession. You can expect support for qualifications and training and your work will be interesting and stretching.

      Swydd Ddisgrifiad

      The People Analytics team provides data-based evidence and insight for the Department's People and Workplace Directorate. We are passionate about innovating and developing our approach to People Analytics across a wide range of important issues of societal and economic interest including, gender pay gap and diversity and inclusion; wellbeing; strategic workforce planning and social mobility.

      As a Band B analyst in the People Analytics team you will take a leading role in supporting policy and operational colleagues within the People and Workplace Directorate You will use your analytical experience to develop work plans and data development projects in close collaboration with senior HR clients, the People Analytics team and policy colleagues.

      We are looking for someone with a strong analytical background, as well as experience in analytical project delivery and presenting complex information to a lay audience.

      Cyfrifoldebau

      -Being comfortable in a diverse policy team, managing competing priorities and leading several projects at any given time
      -Deliver and lead on complex analysis; determine the most appropriate methodology and software approaches, manage resources, priorities and cross-working to ensure timely delivery
      -Work with analyst colleagues who are leading on automation and data-operations to ensure efficient processes for data pipelines into each analysis project.
      -Develop knowledge of the scope and limitations of available data and how it can best be used; provide insight and suggestions for improvements and work with the policy colleagues and senior leadership team to improve data capture and utilisation.
      -Being lead analyst for a number of key HR areas, such as:

      Management Information reporting to Cabinet office and ONS on workforce, sickness absence and locations;
      Deliver monthly Dashboard pack for Executive Board and Senior Leadership Team
      Equality Monitoring - work with staff characteristic data on representation and HR process outcomes to inform and evaluate Diversity and Inclusion work;
      Performance Management - monitor equal treatment of staff across characteristics and business areas;
      Pay and reward - pay modelling and analysis
      People Survey - provide support and analysis of data from staff surveys

      Essential Requirements

      -Good team working skills, with an ability to work autonomously, with pace and with attention to detail
      -Experience of delivering multiple analytical projects to clients who are non-specialists
      -Ability to turn the initial ask into a project specification which meets the real needs of the client
      -Experienced at using analysis to create insight and communicating this effectively to senior decision makers, sometimes at short notice
      -Technical capability to apply data science techniques
      -Ability to improve processes through innovation and automation

      Desirable Skills

      -Undertake univariate and multivariate statistical analysis, cross-sectional analysis, regression and modelling using data tools such as Google sheets, Excel, or R. (Training can be provided on the use of specific tools if required)
      -Work confidently and at pace with disparate, and differently formatted datasets
      -Handle or advise upon the handling and suppression of outputs complicated by small cell counts relating to sensitive personal information such as race and sexual orientation in both administrative and survey data sources
      -Data visualisation skills, and experience of developing a narrative using complex data and interpreting it for different audiences. (This might include use of tools such as Google DataStudios, Microsoft Power BI or R Shiny, though training can be provided on the use of specific software if required)
