About the job

Summary

Ofgem works on behalf of energy consumers to ensure that every household and business in the UK can rely on a safe, affordable and environmentally sustainable energy supply. We are playing a vital part in accelerating the transition to Net Zero and a carbon neutral energy system - a goal that everyone wants to achieve. Whatever your role, you'll be playing your part in creating new energy solutions that are great for customers, and great for the environment.

We offer a diverse range of flexible working career opportunities: roles that are stimulating and rewarding, where you can get involved in ground-breaking work. And it's important to us that we recruit from a wide range of professional and personal backgrounds - bringing different perspectives and experiences to our work.

Effective use of data will be a key tool in a Net Zero energy supply. In this senior role you will take the lead on enhancing data quality and how we make use of it. You'll bring an expert professional insight into how better use of data can facilitate the transition to Net Zero and play a key role in promoting a culture of innovation and excellence in terms of data management. Providing expert data policy advice and guidance to all areas of Ofgem, you'll lead on the creation and roll-out of our data quality framework. Working closely with the Head of Data Governance you'll help to define and embed the vision for this work and put in place the functional strategy, governance framework, organisational constructs and enabling technologies to bring it to life.

It's a role that requires proven expertise in achieving significant data quality improvements and an accomplished understanding of data quality assessment and remediation applications/platforms. Your experience to date will include:
• Data profiling
• Data discovery
• Information chain analysis and management
• Root cause analysis
• Cost-benefit analysis

Your knowledge of Information Management should include strategy, architecture, delivery, and reporting & analytics, as well as an excellent understanding of GDPR and DPA. Your professional expertise should be complemented by highly developed communication and leadership skills, enabling you to get the best from others and to engage, influence and assure stakeholders at all levels. It's important that you can bring a strategic perspective to this role, supported by the practical skills to translate this into practical action on the ground.

Job description

The Analytics and Assurance (A&A) directorate is Ofgem's centre of excellence across specialist disciplines, these include subjects such as Regulatory Finance, Research and Economics, Engineering and, Data & Digital Insights (D&DI). The teams in this directorate advise and support Ofgem's regulatory work across the organisation and provide expert leadership to the energy sector.

This role is in the Data & Digital Insights team where you will work with a wide range of stakeholders - within Ofgem, in government and across the energy industry - to support the digitalisation of the energy sector and to ensure that this is underpinned with data quality best practice and regulation.

The role is responsible for supporting with the development of a data governance framework which may include implementing new policies, standards and procedures that continue to evolve and adapt as required. The role will facilitate with the availability, usability, integrity, and regulatory compliance of Ofgem's data.

You should have a good knowledge of data governance and data digitalisation, with a particular insight into how these can be utilised to improve services for stakeholders, reduce costs and support the transition to Net Zero. You'll be able to work credibly at a senior level and make the connections between strategic priorities and practical actions on the ground. It's also important that you have highly developed stakeholder management, influencing and communication skills.

• The high-level purpose of the role is to support the delivery and embedding of the Data Governance Framework to improve the value, quality, and usability of Ofgems data assets
• As a senior member of the D&DI function you will support the Head of Data Governance in developing the vision and strategy for the function and be accountable for the delivery of data quality aspects of the framework
• The role will play a key role in influencing and shaping the functional strategy, governance framework, organisational constructs and enabling technologies.
• To provide expert data quality advice and guidance to all areas of Ofgem
• You will engage and work with the Head of Function to create an environment that promotes a culture of innovation and Process Excellence through the delivery of increased capability (people, process efficiency and technology).
• The role will provide operational leadership, guidance, oversight, coaching and mentoring to the team and across a data community of interest
• The role is accountable for the operationalising the data quality framework
• The Role actively works with senior business leaders, within multiple forums.
• The role will work with key stakeholders to ensure that we value and manage our data as an asset, can trust it and understand its lineage.

Ofgem is proud to be an equal opportunity employer. We embrace diversity and are committed to creating an inclusive environment for all employees. All employment is decided on the basis of open and fair competition, merit and business need.

Responsibilities

• Responsible for supporting the Head of Function to create, maintain and ensure compliance with the data governance framework across Ofgem
• Responsible for creating and maintaining the overarching data quality policies, standards, processes and controls
• Responsible for driving cultural change, ensuring the data governance framework is understood and embedded across Ofgem
• Responsible for leading initiatives with the data community to identify data quality improvement opportunities and in place the appropriate remediation activities, including changes to standards, processes, controls, and metrics
